# Sourdough Starter

Whole rye flour kickstarts a sluggish culture more reliably than white.
Bakers disagree on whether tap water harms the wild yeasts.
A healthy starter doubles in size within four to six hours of feeding.
An older starter is not necessarily a better starter.
Refrigeration slows fermentation enough for once-weekly feedings.
Texture should resemble thick pancake batter at peak activity.
Some bakers name their starters; others find this faintly embarrassing.

Cable consortia often pool funding across competing tech companies.
Repair ships can take weeks to reach a damaged segment in deep water.
Roughly 99 percent of intercontinental data traffic moves through undersea cables.
Each new cable's route is negotiated across dozens of jurisdictions.
Light pulses inside the fiber travel at roughly two-thirds the speed of light in vacuum.

# Octopus Cognition

Researchers have observed individuals using coconut shells as portable shelters.
Some species appear to recognize individual human handlers.
Two-thirds of an octopus's neurons sit in its arms, not its central brain.
Captive octopuses have repeatedly escaped enclosures by remembering routines.
Each arm can taste, grip, and explore semi-independently of the others.
Their lifespans are short, often under two years, which complicates long studies.
Color-changing skin is striking given that octopuses are likely colorblind.
Problem-solving in cephalopods evolved on a branch entirely separate from vertebrates.

## Hand Tools for Woodworking

Most beginners over-tighten their bench vises and crush soft pine.
Sharpening, more than technique, separates frustration from satisfaction in this craft.
Marking gauges leave a crisper line than any pencil can manage.
A sharp chisel removes more material with less effort than a dull power tool.
A well-tuned hand plane can produce shavings thinner than printer paper.

# Late Roman Currency

Mint marks on bronze coins help modern numismatists trace circulation routes.
Local mints sometimes operated with little oversight from the imperial center.
Constantine's gold solidus held its value for nearly seven centuries.
Silver content in the denarius had collapsed to under five percent by 270 CE.
Diocletian's reforms attempted to stabilize a chaotic third-century coinage.

## Backyard Composting

Worms thrive in cooler, wetter bins than thermophilic piles produce.
Finished compost should smell like forest floor, not like ammonia or rot.
Turning the heap weekly accelerates decomposition and reduces odors.
A balanced pile mixes roughly three parts brown material to one part green.
Citrus peels and onions break down slower than most other kitchen scraps.
Hot composting can reach internal temperatures above 130 degrees Fahrenheit.
Adding too many grass clippings at once creates an anaerobic, slimy mat.

# Long-Distance Hiking

Foot care, not knee care, ends more attempts than anything else.
Water sources in late summer dry up in unpredictable patterns.
Most thru-hikers carry less than fifteen pounds of base weight.
Bear canisters add weight but eliminate the nightly hang.
The first two weeks reshape what a hiker thinks of as comfortable.
